\chapter{Conclusion and Perspectives for the Study of Tricolor}
\label{conclusion}

\section{Final Remarks}

Tricolor is a historically unusual game whose strategic richness had not yet been explored with modern computational tools. This thesis has shown that the game is not only historically interesting, but also computationally complex and strategically nontrivial.

By formalizing the rules, implementing a simulation framework, analyzing game metrics, designing heuristics, and evaluating AI agents, this work provides a first computational foundation for the study of Tricolor. The results suggest that the game offers many opportunities for future research, from stronger AI agents to mathematical analysis and historical interpretation.

More broadly, this thesis illustrates how artificial intelligence can be used not only to play games, but also to study them. In the case of Tricolor, computational methods make it possible to revive and investigate a historical mathematical game that would otherwise remain difficult to analyze systematically. This opens the way for future interdisciplinary work on Tricolor and on other historical games whose strategic properties remain largely unexplored.
